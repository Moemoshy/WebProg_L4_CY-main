Brown Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies

Cookies carry a certain hint of nostaligia in them, whether growing up you eat cookies all the time or not! A cold glass of milk and a plate of cookies has always been enticing to any age. So! Here we will learn how to make a batch of brown butter chocolate chip cookies. The nuttiness of the browned butter adds depth and richness to the already crispy-edged, chewy center, indulgent cookies that you will surely enjoy!


Ingredients

  • ½ C White Sugar
  • ¾ C Brown Sugar
  • 1 tspn Salt
  • ½ C Melted Butter
  • 1 Large Egg
  • 1 tspn Vanilla
  • 1 & ¼ C Flour
  • ½ tspn Baking Soda
  • 4 8 oz Chopped Dark Chocolate
  • 1 tspn Coffee Powder

Equipment

  • Measuring cups
  • Measuring spoons
  • Mixing bowl
  • Spatula
  • Baking sheet/ Baking tray
  • Baking paper/ Baking mat

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375 Fahrenheit (190 Celsius)
  2. Melt the butter in a pan until golden brown and you can smell the nuttiness. Let it cool down.
  3. Mix all the wet ingridients (Butter, Eggs, Sugar [White & Brown], Vanilla) and the salt and coffee powder.
  4. Mix in the flour and baking soda.
  5. Mix in the dark chocolate.
  6. Let it sit out for the butter to solidify a bit, or place in the refrigirator to speed up the process
  7. Roll them up into balls. The size depends on your liking, and space them with atleast 3 inches in between.
  8. Cook in the oven for approximately 12-14 minutes depending on size.
